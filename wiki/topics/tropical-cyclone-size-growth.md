# Definition

Tropical cyclone size growth refers to the radial expansion of the inner core, outer circulation, or damaging-wind envelope as a storm evolves.

# Current Synthesis

This wiki currently has only limited evidence on size growth. [Montgomery and Smith (2017)](../papers/montgomery-smith-2017-fluid-dynamics-of-tropical-cyclones.md) treat size growth as a relevant part of tropical cyclone fluid dynamics, but not as a fully resolved theory.

Within the review, one recurring idea is that size change is coupled to boundary-layer and diabatic-heating structure. The paper discusses model behavior in which expansion of swirling wind in the lower troposphere leads to larger radii of forced eyewall ascent and a reduction in maximum tangential wind, while both inner-core and outer-circulation size increase.

# Evidence By Source

- Montgomery and Smith (2017) list size growth as a central keyword of the review.
- The review describes model results in which decay after mature intensity is accompanied by growth of both the inner-core radius and the radius of gale-force winds.
- The authors explain that behavior in terms of a boundary-layer-control mechanism: lower-tropospheric expansion of swirling wind changes the radii of forced eyewall ascent and modifies inflow through associated changes in diabatic-heating structure.
- The paper references additional size-focused studies, but those primary papers have not yet been ingested into this wiki.

# Open Questions

- What sets outer-wind expansion separately from inner-core expansion?
- How tightly linked are size growth and intensity change?
- Which size mechanisms survive when moving from axisymmetric experiments to realistic 3D and coupled simulations?
