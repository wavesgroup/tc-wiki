# Definition

Tropical cyclone outflow is the upper-tropospheric divergent circulation and associated cirrus cloud shield that export mass and condensate away from the storm's deep convection.

# Current Synthesis

This wiki currently has one direct primary paper focused on outflow structure. [Nolan et al. (2025)](../papers/nolan-fischer-oneill-2025-mass-and-condensate-sources-for-tropical-cyclone-outflow.md) argue that the standard conceptual model, in which the eyewall supplies nearly all of the upper-level outflow and cirrus shield, is incomplete.

In their simulations of a hurricane nature run and Hurricane Laura (2020), the surrounding rainbands contribute a large share of the outflow dry-air mass flux and an even larger share of the condensate in the cirrus shield. The paper shows that this supply occurs through both narrow convective towers and broader mesoscale ascending updrafts. As a result, changes in cirrus-shield size and thickness should not be interpreted as a direct measure of eyewall forcing alone.

# Evidence By Source

- [Nolan et al. (2025)](../papers/nolan-fischer-oneill-2025-mass-and-condensate-sources-for-tropical-cyclone-outflow.md) diagnose the outflow layer mainly between about 100 and 300 km radius and find that rainbands provide a substantial fraction of the dry-air mass flux.
- The same paper finds that rainbands provide most of the condensate in the outflow cloud shield in their two simulations because eyewall condensate falls out rapidly and the eyewall occupies a relatively small radial area.
- The authors identify two rainband pathways into the outflow: deep convective towers with strong local ascent and mesoscale sloping updrafts with weaker but broader ascent.
- The paper argues that the size and thickness of the cirrus shield therefore reflect rainband activity as well as eyewall behavior, which matters for interpreting satellite-based intensity diagnostics.
- Nolan et al. (2025) also argue that frequent rainband injections make the outflow less self-contained than many standard schematics imply.

# Open Questions

- How often do rainbands dominate outflow mass and condensate budgets across basins, shear regimes, and storm intensities?
- Can aircraft or satellite datasets close the outflow mass and condensate budgets observationally rather than only infer the structure qualitatively?
- How should outflow diagnostics used in intensity estimation separate eyewall signals from rainband contributions?
