# Definition

Secondary eyewall formation refers to the development of an outer concentric ring of strong convection, vorticity, and tangential wind around an existing primary eyewall, usually separated by a moat and often preceding an eyewall replacement cycle.

# Current Synthesis

This is currently a first-pass page built from one direct primary study. [Judt and Chen (2010)](../papers/judt-chen-2010-rainband-pv-secondary-eyewall-rita.md) compare Hurricanes Rita and Katrina (2005) during the RAINEX period and argue that Rita's secondary eyewall formed when convectively generated potential vorticity (PV) accumulated in the rainband region, creating a secondary PV/vorticity maximum that later became the outer eyewall.

The paper further argues that a strong moat formed between Rita's primary eyewall and the outer convective ring, suppressing radial propagation of vortex Rossby waves (VRWs). In that interpretation, VRWs were not the initiating cause of secondary-eyewall formation in Rita. Katrina, by contrast, retained outward-propagating VRWs without forming a comparable secondary eyewall during the same analysis window.

Inference for this wiki: secondary-eyewall formation should be treated as an outer-core organization problem as much as an inner-core wave problem. Rainband convection, PV aggregation, and moat structure are explicit candidates for first-order control, but this page remains thin until more concentric-eyewall papers are ingested.

# Evidence By Source

- [Judt and Chen (2010)](../papers/judt-chen-2010-rainband-pv-secondary-eyewall-rita.md) diagnose stronger rainband-region PV generation and accumulation in Rita than in Katrina.
- The same paper shows that Rita developed a secondary PV/vorticity maximum that later became the secondary eyewall.
- Judt and Chen (2010) argue that a strong moat with near-zero PV gradient inhibited VRW propagation in Rita, so VRWs were not the initial trigger of the secondary eyewall.
- The Katrina comparison implies that outward-propagating VRWs alone are not sufficient for secondary-eyewall formation in this framework.

# Open Questions

- How general is the rainband-PV pathway across storms, basins, and model systems?
- When do VRWs materially contribute after the initial secondary ring begins to form?
- Which observable precursors best distinguish outer rainbands that will organize into a secondary eyewall from those that will not?
- How tightly coupled are secondary-eyewall formation, storm-size growth, and short-term intensity change?
