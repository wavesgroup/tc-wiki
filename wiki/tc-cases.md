# Wiki Tropical Cyclone Cases Index

This page indexes specific named historical tropical cyclones materially discussed on current wiki pages. Entries are grouped by basin, then by storm year in ascending order, with cases alphabetized within each year.

Basin jump: [North Atlantic](#north-atlantic)

## North Atlantic

### 2005

- **Katrina**: Category 5 North Atlantic hurricane that crossed the Bahamas and Gulf of Mexico and made U.S. landfalls in South Florida and along the Louisiana-Mississippi coast. Comparison case in RAINEX used to contrast Rita's outer-core evolution and show that outward-propagating vortex Rossby waves alone did not produce a comparable secondary eyewall. [Judt and Chen (2010)](papers/judt-chen-2010-rainband-pv-secondary-eyewall-rita.md); [Secondary Eyewall Formation](topics/secondary-eyewall-formation.md)
- **Rita**: Category 5 North Atlantic hurricane that crossed the Florida Straits and Gulf of Mexico and made landfall in southwestern Louisiana near the Texas border. Primary secondary-eyewall formation case showing rainband PV accumulation, moat development, and outer-core reorganization during mature intensity change. [Judt and Chen (2010)](papers/judt-chen-2010-rainband-pv-secondary-eyewall-rita.md); [Secondary Eyewall Formation](topics/secondary-eyewall-formation.md); [Tropical Cyclone Intensification](topics/tropical-cyclone-intensification.md); [Tropical Cyclone Size Growth](topics/tropical-cyclone-size-growth.md); [Tropical Cyclone Fluid Dynamics](syntheses/tropical-cyclone-fluid-dynamics.md)

### 2008

- **Ike**: Category 4 North Atlantic hurricane that crossed the Caribbean and Gulf of Mexico and made landfall at Galveston, Texas. Gulf landfall wave case used to show shallow-water spectral broadening, strong wind-wave misalignment, and wave-coupled stress changes near the Texas coast. [Chen and Curcic (2016)](papers/chen-curcic-2016-ocean-surface-waves-ike-sandy.md); [Tropical Cyclone Wave Coupling](topics/tropical-cyclone-wave-coupling.md)

### 2010

- **Earl**: Category 4 North Atlantic hurricane that recurved over the western Atlantic and later made landfall in Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island. Single-storm coupled predictability and RI-timing case used to separate fast convective error growth, internal-vortex uncertainty, and stochastic environmental forcing. [Judt et al. (2016)](papers/judt-chen-berner-2016-intensity-predictability-scale-dependent-error-growth.md); [Judt and Chen (2016)](papers/judt-chen-2016-ri-predictability-stochastic-ensembles.md); [Li et al. (2019)](papers/li-et-al-2019-uncertainty-propagation-coupled-atmosphere-wave-ocean-earl.md); [Rapid Intensification](topics/rapid-intensification.md); [Tropical Cyclone Predictability](topics/tropical-cyclone-predictability.md); [Tropical Cyclone Numerical Modeling](topics/tropical-cyclone-numerical-modeling.md)

### 2012

- **Isaac**: Category 1 North Atlantic hurricane that moved through the Caribbean and Gulf of Mexico and made landfall in southeastern Louisiana after crossing Haiti and Cuba. Large slow-moving Gulf case used to diagnose hurricane Stokes-drift transport effects and the sensitivity of storm-surge forecasts to atmospheric forcing choice. [Curcic et al. (2016)](papers/curcic-chen-ozgokmen-2016-hurricane-induced-ocean-waves-stokes-drift.md); [Dietrich et al. (2018)](papers/dietrich-et-al-2018-sensitivity-storm-surge-predictions-isaac.md); [Air-Sea Interaction](topics/air-sea-interaction.md); [Tropical Cyclone Wave Coupling](topics/tropical-cyclone-wave-coupling.md)
- **Sandy**: Category 3 North Atlantic hurricane that formed in the southwestern Caribbean Sea and made hurricane landfalls in Jamaica and Cuba before post-tropical landfall in New Jersey. Landfall wave-structure case used to compare coupled wave predictions and show strong nearshore spectral broadening and wind-wave misalignment along the northeastern U.S. coast. [Chen and Curcic (2016)](papers/chen-curcic-2016-ocean-surface-waves-ike-sandy.md); [Tropical Cyclone Wave Coupling](topics/tropical-cyclone-wave-coupling.md)

### 2014

- **Gonzalo**: Category 4 North Atlantic hurricane that formed east of the Lesser Antilles and made landfall in Bermuda. High-resolution ensemble ventilation case used to diagnose tilt, dry-air intrusion, and RI-timing spread in one real-storm event. [Fischer et al. (2023)](papers/fischer-et-al-2023-two-vortex-evolutions-ventilation-ri.md)

### 2017

- **Harvey**: Category 4 North Atlantic hurricane that regenerated in the Bay of Campeche and made landfall on the middle Texas coast. Targeted 2017 Atlantic forecast case used in FV3/HifvGFS event-level diagnostics. [Hazelton et al. (2018)](papers/hazelton-et-al-2018-2017-atlantic-hurricane-forecasts-high-resolution-fvgfs.md)
- **Irma**: Category 5 Cape Verde hurricane in the North Atlantic that crossed the northeastern Caribbean and made landfall in the Florida Keys and southwestern Florida. Florida Reef Tract transport case showing that wave radiation stress and especially Stokes drift can strongly redirect drifting material during a major hurricane. [Dobbelaere et al. (2022)](papers/dobbelaere-curcic-lehenaff-hanert-2022-wave-induced-ocean-transport-irma.md); [Tropical Cyclone Wave Coupling](topics/tropical-cyclone-wave-coupling.md)
- **Maria**: Category 5 Cape Verde hurricane in the North Atlantic that crossed the northeastern Caribbean Sea and made destructive landfalls in Dominica and Puerto Rico. 2017 Atlantic recurvature case used to diagnose positive intensity bias and the likely value of ocean coupling in FV3/HifvGFS. [Hazelton et al. (2018)](papers/hazelton-et-al-2018-2017-atlantic-hurricane-forecasts-high-resolution-fvgfs.md)

### 2018

- **Michael**: Category 5 North Atlantic hurricane that rapidly intensified over the eastern Gulf of Mexico and made catastrophic landfall near Mexico Beach, Florida. Sheared rapid-intensification case used to diagnose tilt reduction, upshear humidification, and ensemble spread in RI outcomes. [Hazelton et al. (2020)](papers/hazelton-et-al-2020-high-resolution-ensemble-hfv3-hurricane-michael-ri-shear.md); [Rapid Intensification](topics/rapid-intensification.md); [Tropical Cyclone Predictability](topics/tropical-cyclone-predictability.md); [Tropical Cyclone Numerical Modeling](topics/tropical-cyclone-numerical-modeling.md)

### 2020

- **Laura**: Category 4 North Atlantic hurricane that crossed the Greater Antilles and Gulf of Mexico and made landfall near Cameron, Louisiana. Mature-outflow case showing that surrounding rainbands can supply large fractions of outflow dry mass and cirrus-shield condensate. [Nolan et al. (2025)](papers/nolan-fischer-oneill-2025-mass-and-condensate-sources-for-tropical-cyclone-outflow.md); [Tropical Cyclone Outflow](topics/tropical-cyclone-outflow.md)
