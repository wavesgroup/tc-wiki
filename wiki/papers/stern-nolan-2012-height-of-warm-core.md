# Research Question

Stern and Nolan (2012) ask where the warm-core temperature maximum is typically located in tropical cyclones and whether warm-core height varies systematically with intensity, storm size, and model-physics choices.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Idealized numerical-model process study.
- Model platform: Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) simulations in controlled tropical-cyclone configurations.
- Experiment set (from accessible abstract record): Control simulation plus sensitivity tests varying initial vortex size and microphysics parameterization.
- Diagnostics: Height of maximum perturbation temperature and its relation to intensity and structural evolution.

# Findings

- In the accessible abstract record, the control simulation places the warm-core maximum in the midtroposphere (roughly 5-6 km), not in the upper troposphere.
- The authors argue that the widely cited upper-level warm-core archetype was based on a limited number of case studies and may not be broadly representative.
- Across their sensitivity tests, warm-core height is reported near 4-8 km and relatively insensitive to intensity over a broad storm-strength range.
