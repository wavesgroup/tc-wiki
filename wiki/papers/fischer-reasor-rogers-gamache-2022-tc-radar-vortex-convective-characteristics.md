# Research Question

Fischer et al. (2022) ask how three-dimensional inner-core vortex and convective structure vary as a function of tropical-cyclone intensity when sampled with a consistent airborne Doppler-radar framework.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Multibasin observational analysis and database-introduction paper.
- Database: TC-RADAR (Tropical Cyclone Radar Archive of Doppler Analyses with Re-centering).
- Sampling scope: More than 900 analyses from 273 aircraft flights spanning 1997-2020 across the North Atlantic, eastern North Pacific, and central North Pacific.
- Structural diagnostics: Vorticity radial profile shape, vortex tilt, decay of circulation with height, secondary circulation, and vertical-velocity distributions by intensity class.

# Findings

- Weaker storms commonly exhibit monopolar vorticity profiles and broader spread in tilt magnitude.
- With increasing intensity, the vorticity maximum shifts toward an annular structure inside the radius of peak wind, and the vortex decays more slowly with height.
- Stronger storms are, on average, more vertically aligned.
- The secondary circulation strengthens and shallows with intensity, including stronger lower-tropospheric inflow and larger azimuthal-mean ascent.
- Vertical-velocity distributions vary systematically with storm intensity, radial domain, and height, providing a baseline for later RI-focused analyses.

# Limitations

- Despite broad coverage, aircraft sampling remains opportunistic and uneven across basins, seasons, and storm lifecycle stages.
- The paper emphasizes composite structural relationships with intensity, not RI-onset causality.
- Radar retrieval limits persist in the lowest boundary layer and in some upper-level domains with sparse scatterers.
