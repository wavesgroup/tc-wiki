# Research Question

Cione and Uhlhorn (2003) evaluate how SST varies within the hurricane environment and how that variability is related to short-term intensity change.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Observational analysis of hurricane-environment SST variability.
- Variables emphasized: Along-track and near-core SST structure, wake cooling signatures, and associated intensity tendency context.
- Goal: Determine whether unresolved SST heterogeneity can degrade intensity interpretation and prediction.

# Findings

- Cione and Uhlhorn (2003) found substantial SST variability in and around tropical cyclones, including wake-related cooling signals relevant to subsequent intensity evolution.
- The study argued that representing storm-relative SST structure, not just coarse background SST, is important for interpreting intensity change.
- Inference for this wiki: observationally constrained wake variability helps explain why coupled forecast skill depends strongly on ocean initial conditions.

# Limitations

- This ingest is first-pass and should be upgraded with direct extraction of sampling strategy and quantitative SST-intensity relationships from full text.
- Observational sampling limitations in extreme conditions can affect representativeness of inner-core SST estimates.
