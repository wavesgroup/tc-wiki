# Research Question

Donelan et al. (2004) ask whether ocean aerodynamic roughness and drag continue to increase without bound at hurricane-force winds.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Controlled high-wind air-sea interaction analysis (laboratory-focused with hurricane-wind relevance).
- Diagnostics: Aerodynamic roughness and drag-coefficient estimates under very strong wind forcing.
- Context: Designed as a physical-process test to complement sparse open-ocean hurricane observations.

# Findings

- The paper reports a limiting behavior in ocean aerodynamic roughness at very high winds.
- Consistent with that roughness limit, momentum drag does not continue to increase indefinitely in the strongest wind regime.
- The result became a central reference for high-wind drag saturation discussions and later reassessments.

# Limitations
