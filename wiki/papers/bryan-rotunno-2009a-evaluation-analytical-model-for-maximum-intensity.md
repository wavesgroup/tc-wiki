# Citation

Bryan, George H., and Richard Rotunno, 2009a: *Evaluation of an Analytical Model for the Maximum Intensity of Tropical Cyclones*. *Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences*, 66, 3042-3060. DOI: [10.1175/2009JAS3038.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/2009JAS3038.1).

Accessed text sources: [AMS journal page](https://journals.ametsoc.org/view/journals/atsc/66/10/2009jas3038.1.xml) and [Montgomery and Smith (2017) review synthesis](montgomery-smith-2017-fluid-dynamics-of-tropical-cyclones.md).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary numerical/theoretical research article.

# Research Question

Bryan and Rotunno (2009a) test how well analytical PI-style intensity models match tropical-cyclone maximum intensity behavior in controlled numerical-model settings.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Theory-testing numerical-model evaluation.
- Framework: Axisymmetric idealized simulations compared against analytical model predictions.
- Diagnostics: Maximum wind/intensity sensitivity to exchange assumptions and thermodynamic formulation details.
- Comparison goal: Identify which analytical assumptions reproduce simulated maximum intensity and which fail.

# Findings

- Bryan and Rotunno (2009a) provide a direct evaluation framework for PI-style analytical models rather than assuming those models hold a priori.
- The study is widely cited as a key test bed for identifying where analytical maximum-intensity models align with or diverge from axisymmetric simulation behavior.
- Inference from [Montgomery and Smith (2017)](montgomery-smith-2017-fluid-dynamics-of-tropical-cyclones.md): results in this testing chain support caution that PI skill depends strongly on model assumptions and parameter choices.

# Limitations

- Current ingest is a first pass from bibliographic and review-level evidence; full experiment matrix and quantitative diagnostics should be extracted from full text in a follow-up pass.
- Axisymmetric simulation testing cannot fully represent 3-D convective organization and eddy momentum transport.
- Interpretation depends on the specific numerical setup and parameterization choices explored in the paper.
