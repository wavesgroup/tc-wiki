# Citation

Fischer, M. S., B. H. Tang, and K. L. Corbosiero, 2019: *A climatological analysis of tropical cyclone rapid intensification in environments of upper-tropospheric troughs*. *Monthly Weather Review*, 147, 3693-3719. DOI: [10.1175/MWR-D-19-0013.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/MWR-D-19-0013.1).

Accessed text source: [DOI landing page](https://doi.org/10.1175/MWR-D-19-0013.1).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Fischer et al. (2019) ask which tropical-cyclone/upper-tropospheric-trough configurations are most favorable for rapid intensification and why.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: North Atlantic climatological composite study of TC-trough interactions.
- Classification method: Dimensionality reduction plus k-means clustering to partition trough configurations.
- Diagnostics: Upper-level potential-vorticity structure, convective characteristics, and environmental ventilation metrics for RI versus non-RI episodes.

# Findings

- RI episodes are associated with shorter-wavelength upper-tropospheric trough structures and larger upstream TC-trough displacements than non-RI episodes.
- RI occurs most frequently when an upper-level cutoff low is located roughly 500-1000 km southwest of the TC center.
- RI-favorable TC-trough environments are linked to reduced ventilation of the warm core by low-entropy environmental air.
- The authors find little evidence that trough-induced eddy angular-momentum flux convergence alone explains RI occurrence.
- RI episodes are still associated with anomalously vigorous inner-core convection.

# Limitations

- The analysis is climatological and basin-specific (North Atlantic), so transferability to other basins is not automatic.
- Composite methods identify typical configurations but cannot fully resolve event-level causality.
- Results depend on objective clustering and metric definitions for interaction geometry.
