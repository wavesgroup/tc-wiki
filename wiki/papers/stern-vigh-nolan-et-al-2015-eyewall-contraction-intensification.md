Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Stern et al. (2015) reevaluate whether eyewall (RMW) contraction and tropical-cyclone intensification remain tightly coupled throughout the full intensification lifecycle.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Idealized numerical-experiment analysis of inner-core structural evolution.
- Core diagnostics (from accessible abstract text): Time evolution of RMW contraction and maximum-wind intensification rates across storms with varying initial size.
- Conceptual framing: Test of the convective-ring view in which contraction and intensification are expected to occur together.

# Findings

- The accessible abstract record indicates that contraction and intensification begin together in idealized simulations but decouple later, with contraction ending well before peak intensity.
- Reported contraction rates decrease with larger initial storm size, while intensification rates do not show the same systematic size dependence.
- This implies that continued intensification can proceed after major geometric contraction slows or halts, refining simple one-to-one contraction-intensification narratives.

# Limitations

- Full-text extraction from the AMS article is still needed for exact experiment design, diagnostics, and uncertainty language.
- This first-pass ingest depends on bibliographic abstract records and should be treated as a constrained synthesis draft.
- Idealized-model results may not transfer directly to all observed eyewall evolution pathways, including eyewall replacement and strongly sheared environments.
