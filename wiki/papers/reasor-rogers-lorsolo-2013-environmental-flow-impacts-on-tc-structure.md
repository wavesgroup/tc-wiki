# Citation

Reasor, P., R. Rogers, and S. Lorsolo, 2013: *Environmental flow impacts on tropical cyclone structure diagnosed from airborne Doppler radar composites*. *Monthly Weather Review*, 141(9), 2949-2969. DOI: [10.1175/MWR-D-12-00334.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/MWR-D-12-00334.1).

Accessed text source: [DOI landing page](https://doi.org/10.1175/MWR-D-12-00334.1).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Reasor et al. (2013) ask how environmental flow and shear context map onto systematic inner-core structure differences in storms sampled by airborne Doppler radar.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Multi-case airborne Doppler radar composite analysis.
- Platform: NOAA aircraft Doppler-radar analyses.
- Focus: Shear-relative structure and environmental-flow controls on vortex organization.

# Findings

- Environmental flow systematically modulates observed inner-core structure.
- The analysis supports treating shear interaction as a structure-and-evolution problem, not a single-threshold predictor.
- Composite airborne radar diagnostics provide a bridge between case-process studies and larger-sample structural inference.

# Limitations

- Composite methods can smooth storm-specific process details.
- Airborne sampling remains limited to reconnaissance-targeted storms and periods.
- The paper diagnoses structural relationships more directly than causal forecast interventions.
