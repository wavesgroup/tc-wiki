# Research Question

Wright et al. (2001) ask how tropical-cyclone directional wave spectra vary spatially in open-ocean storm environments when sampled with airborne remote sensing.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Hurricane wave-field observational analysis.
- Measurement platform context: Airborne radar-altimeter lineage cited as predecessor to later NOAA WSRA operations.
- Core diagnostic: Two-dimensional directional wave spectra and their storm-relative spatial variability in open-ocean conditions.

# Findings

- The study established an early open-ocean observational benchmark for spatially heterogeneous directional wave spectra around tropical cyclones.
- Later papers treat this dataset as foundational evidence that wave-field structure depends on storm-relative position, not only local wind speed magnitude.
- The paper helped motivate subsequent airborne systems designed for denser storm-scale sampling of wave directionality and partitioned wave systems.
