# Research Question

Reasor and Montgomery (2015) evaluate whether a simplified heuristic resilience model can explain and organize observed/model behavior of tropical cyclones subjected to vertical shear.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Dynamical theory/model-evaluation paper.
- Framework: Heuristic resilience model tested against known TC-shear behavior.
- Focus: How vortex structure, shear forcing, and alignment dynamics jointly set resilience outcomes.

# Findings

- The heuristic model captures first-order differences in resilience behavior across shear-vortex regimes.
- Results support a process view in which resilience is controlled by coupled vortex structure and environmental forcing, not by shear magnitude alone.
- The paper helps formalize a practical conceptual bridge between idealized resilience theory and real-storm interpretation.

# Limitations

- Heuristic reduction cannot resolve full moist multiscale physics.
- Performance depends on the validity of structural assumptions inherited from prior theory and case evidence.
- The framework is diagnostic/conceptual rather than an operational intensity model.
