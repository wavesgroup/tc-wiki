# Citation

Zhang, J. A., P. G. Black, J. R. French, and W. M. Drennan, 2008: *First Direct Measurements of Enthalpy Flux in the Hurricane Boundary Layer: The CBLAST Results*. *Geophysical Research Letters*, 35(11), L14813. DOI: [10.1029/2008GL034374](https://doi.org/10.1029/2008GL034374).

Accessed text sources: [NOAA/AOML Hurricane Research Division publications page](https://www.aoml.noaa.gov/hrd/about_hrd/publications.html) and DOI metadata via the [publisher landing page](https://doi.org/10.1029/2008GL034374).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Zhang et al. (2008) ask how sensible heat and total enthalpy flux behave in the hurricane boundary layer, and what direct flux measurements imply for the exchange-coefficient ratio relevant to hurricane development theory.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Direct observational flux analysis.
- Program context: CBLAST-hurricane aircraft flights designed to measure turbulent air-sea exchange in strong winds.
- Wind regime: 10-m wind speeds up to about 30 m s^-1.
- Diagnostics: Sensible heat flux, total enthalpy flux, bulk exchange coefficients for enthalpy, and the ratio of enthalpy to momentum exchange coefficients.

# Findings

- Zhang et al. (2008) report the first field observations of sensible heat and enthalpy flux in the hurricane boundary layer.
- The paper reports no statistically significant dependence of the relevant bulk exchange coefficients on wind speed over the observed range.
- The authors find a mean ratio of the exchange coefficient for enthalpy to that for momentum that is significantly below the lowest threshold estimated by previous investigators.
- The paper argues that this result implies either an additional enthalpy source, such as sea spray or lateral fluxes from the vortex warm core, or a need to re-evaluate the theoretical threshold itself.

# Limitations

- The observed regime extends only to about 30 m s^-1, so the paper does not directly resolve major-hurricane exchange behavior.
- The CK/CD interpretation depends on the theoretical thresholds used for comparison, so the mechanistic implication is not unique.
- This short GRL paper is best read as a direct observational constraint rather than a complete theory of hurricane maintenance.
