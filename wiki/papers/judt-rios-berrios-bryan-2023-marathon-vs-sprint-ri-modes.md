# Citation

Judt, F., R. Rios-Berrios, and G. H. Bryan, 2023: *Marathon vs. Sprint: Two modes of tropical cyclone rapid intensification in a global convection-permitting simulation*. *Monthly Weather Review*, 151(10), 2683-2699. DOI: [10.1175/MWR-D-23-0038.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/MWR-D-23-0038.1).

Accessed text source: [Author-posted PDF copy](https://falkojudt.github.io/files/mwre-MWR-D-23-0038.1.compressed.pdf).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Judt et al. (2023) ask whether rapid intensification (RI) occurs in more than one structural mode, and whether distinct RI modes can help explain why the RI literature disagrees about the roles of convective bursts, symmetry, and environmental shear.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Global convection-permitting simulation analysis with observational cross-check.
- Model framework: A 40-day global convection-permitting simulation that produced 23 tropical cyclones, 7 of which underwent RI.
- Comparative design: Marathon-mode and sprint-mode RI archetypes identified from the simulation, then compared by RI duration, vortex structure, and environmental conditions.
- External comparison: HURDAT2 best-track data used to test whether RI-duration-based mode distinctions also appear in observations.
- Diagnostics: RI-period duration, vortex symmetry and center definition, convective-burst behavior, mesovortex/center-reformation sequence, vertical wind shear, and low-level relative humidity.

# Findings

- The study identifies two RI modes. The marathon mode consists of a gradual, sustained intensification period, whereas the sprint mode consists of sudden, short-lived RI bursts.
- HURDAT2-based RI-duration criteria also support the existence of both modes, suggesting the simulation's two-mode result is not purely a model artifact.
- Marathon-mode cases begin RI with well-defined, symmetric vortices and occur in relatively favorable low-shear environments; all marathon cases in the simulation became major hurricanes.
- Sprint-mode cases begin with poorly defined, asymmetric vortices and intensify through a convective-burst-driven sequence in which a downshear burst spawns a mesovortex and center reformation; these cases occur in stronger shear and drier environments and reach lower post-RI peak intensity.
- Judt et al. (2023) argue that the existence of two RI modes can reconcile why some studies emphasize symmetric spinup in weak shear while others document burst-driven RI under less favorable conditions.

# Limitations

- The analysis is anchored to one 40-day simulation and only seven RI events, with detailed process diagnosis centered on two archetypes.
- Mode membership is presented as a spectrum rather than a rigid binary classification, so case boundaries remain somewhat subjective.
- Environmental and structural differences may depend on the specific global convection-permitting model configuration.
- Further observational and theoretical validation is still needed, especially for the burst-driven sprint mode.
