# Citation

Reasor, P. D., and M. D. Eastin, 2009: *Rapidly intensifying Hurricane Guillermo (1997). Part I: Low-wavenumber structure and evolution*. *Monthly Weather Review*, 137(2), 603-631. DOI: [10.1175/2008MWR2407.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/2008MWR2407.1).

Accessed text source: [DOI landing page](https://doi.org/10.1175/2008MWR2407.1).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Reasor and Eastin (2009) ask how low-wavenumber inner-core asymmetries evolved during Hurricane Guillermo (1997) as the storm rapidly intensified.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Aircraft-observational structural diagnosis.
- Case: Hurricane Guillermo (1997) during rapid-intensification period.
- Focus: Wavenumber-decomposed vortex and thermodynamic structure, including shear-relative asymmetry.

# Findings

- The paper documents coherent low-wavenumber structural evolution during RI rather than purely random convective noise.
- Shear-relative asymmetries and vortex structural adjustment are analyzed as part of Guillermo's RI pathway.
- The Part I diagnosis sets up Part II's explicit resilience-in-shear interpretation.

# Limitations

- Single-storm case-study design limits basin- and regime-generalization.
- Findings are tied to available aircraft sampling windows during one RI event.
- Mechanistic interpretation still depends partly on companion and follow-on studies.
