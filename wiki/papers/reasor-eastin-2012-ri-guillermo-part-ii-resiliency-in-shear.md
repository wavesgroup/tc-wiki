# Citation

Reasor, P. D., and M. D. Eastin, 2012: *Rapidly intensifying Hurricane Guillermo (1997), Part II: Resiliency in shear*. *Monthly Weather Review*, 140(2), 425-444. DOI: [10.1175/MWR-D-11-00080.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/MWR-D-11-00080.1).

Accessed text source: [DOI landing page](https://doi.org/10.1175/MWR-D-11-00080.1).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Reasor and Eastin (2012) ask how Guillermo maintained and reorganized its core while rapidly intensifying in a sheared environment, and what that implies for vortex resiliency concepts.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Aircraft-observational hurricane case study with dynamical interpretation.
- Case: Hurricane Guillermo (1997) rapid-intensification period.
- Approach: Shear-relative structural analyses of vortex evolution and asymmetry.

# Findings

- Guillermo remained resilient in moderate shear while intensifying rapidly.
- Observed evolution is interpreted as evidence that vortex resilience depends on both environmental forcing and internal structural adjustment.
- The paper links real-storm RI behavior to the broader resiliency framework developed in earlier theory work.

# Limitations

- The study is centered on one eastern Pacific hurricane.
- Aircraft sampling does not observe all scales continuously.
- Findings are process rich but not by themselves a probabilistic RI forecast model.
