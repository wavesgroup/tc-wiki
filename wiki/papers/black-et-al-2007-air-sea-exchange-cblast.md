# Research Question

Black et al. (2007) synthesize what the 2002-04 CBLAST-hurricane field program added to hurricane air-sea interaction science, with emphasis on turbulent exchange coefficients, coherent boundary-layer structures, upper-ocean response, and directional wave observations.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Field-program synthesis, not a single new experiment.
- Atmospheric observations synthesized: Low-level aircraft flux legs and related boundary-layer measurements from Hurricanes Fabian (2003) and Isabel (2003).
- Ocean observations synthesized: Arrays of drifters and subsurface floats deployed ahead of Fabian (2003) and Frances (2004), plus surface-wave observations.
- Core diagnostics: Drag coefficient, Dalton number, implied moisture-to-drag ratio, roll-vortex wavelength, mixed-layer deepening, SST cooling, inertial currents, and significant wave height.

# Findings

- Black et al. (2007) report that CBLAST extended the wind-speed range of directly derived momentum and moisture exchange coefficients by about 30% and 60%, respectively.
- The synthesis reports drag-coefficient behavior becoming approximately invariant above about 23 m s^-1, with values above that threshold lower than in earlier open-ocean measurements.
- The paper reports Dalton-number estimates that remain approximately invariant up to 30 m s^-1, implying a CE/CD ratio near 0.7 in the directly observed regime.
- The authors argue that this modest CE/CD ratio implies either additional energy pathways for hurricane intensification or deficiencies in then-common theoretical threshold assumptions.
- The synthesis identifies roll-vortex or linear coherent features with wavelengths of about 0.9-1.2 km and suggests that these structures may augment moisture transport in the hurricane boundary layer.
- On the ocean side, the paper reports that Frances (2004) observations captured rapid mixed-layer deepening from about 40 to 120 m, right-rear SST cooling of about 3.2 C in 11 h, inertial currents peaking near 1.5 m s^-1, and significant waves near 11 m.

# Limitations

- This is a synthesis article, so many conclusions are distilled from multiple CBLAST components rather than derived from one uniform analysis framework.
- The direct turbulent-flux branch summarized here is still based mainly on moderate-to-minimal hurricane wind speeds, not the most extreme major-hurricane regime.
- The major-hurricane CK and CK/CD question remained open in this synthesis and was addressed later by [Bell et al. (2012)](bell-montgomery-emanuel-2012-major-hurricane-exchange-coefficients-cblast.md).
