Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary coupled atmosphere-wave-ocean modeling and drifter-analysis study.

# Research Question

Curcic et al. (2016) ask how hurricane-induced surface waves and Stokes drift modify near-surface transport and lateral dispersion during Hurricane Isaac (2012) in the Gulf of Mexico.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Coupled-model and observational transport analysis.
- Case: Hurricane Isaac (2012) during the Grand Lagrangian Deployment (GLAD), with 194 surface drifters sampling storm-forced near-surface flow.
- Model framework: The fully coupled UWIN-CM atmosphere-wave-ocean system, used to diagnose Eulerian currents, wave fields, and 1 m averaged Stokes drift.
- Diagnostics: Drifter trajectories, Lagrangian velocity, storm-relative Stokes-drift structure, and scale-dependent relative diffusivity.

# Findings

- Curcic et al. (2016) found that hurricane-induced Stokes drift materially altered Isaac's near-surface transport, producing cyclonic rotational flow on the left side of the track and anticyclonic rotational flow on the right side.
- The paper reports that Stokes drift contributed about 20% of the average Lagrangian velocity during strong forcing and changed flow orientation by up to about 20 degrees, so Eulerian currents alone underestimated observed surface transport.
- The GLAD drifters and coupled-model analysis showed much stronger lateral spreading during and after Isaac than before the storm, with scale-dependent relative diffusivity estimated to be about six times larger than under prestorm conditions.

# Limitations

- The ocean-model resolution was too coarse to resolve all of the submesoscale dispersion seen by the drifters, so relative spreading after the storm was underpredicted.
- This is a single-storm transport study centered on the uppermost meter sampled by GLAD-like drifters, not a broad climatology across storms or basins.
- The paper emphasized advective transport from Eulerian currents plus diagnosed Stokes drift; additional wave-current interaction pathways beyond that decomposition were not the central closure target here.
