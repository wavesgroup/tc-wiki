# Citation

French, J. F., W. M. Drennan, J. A. Zhang, and P. G. Black, 2007: *Turbulent Fluxes in the Hurricane Boundary Layer. Part I: Momentum Flux*. *Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences*, 64(4), 1089-1102. DOI: [10.1175/JAS3887.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/JAS3887.1).

Accessed text sources: [NOAA/AOML Hurricane Research Division publications page](https://www.aoml.noaa.gov/hrd/about_hrd/publications.html) and DOI metadata via the [publisher landing page](https://doi.org/10.1175/JAS3887.1).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

French et al. (2007) ask how momentum flux and the effective drag coefficient behave in the hurricane boundary layer when measured directly from low-level aircraft turbulence data.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Direct observational turbulence analysis.
- Storm sample: Hurricanes Fabian (2003) and Isabel (2003).
- Platform: Specially instrumented NOAA P-3 aircraft.
- Sampling: 48 flux legs flown within about 400 m of the sea surface in regions with surface winds between about 18 and 30 m s^-1.
- Primary diagnostics: Surface wind stress and drag coefficient inferred from direct momentum-flux measurements.

# Findings

- French et al. (2007) report the first direct measurements of momentum flux from within hurricane boundary layers.
- The paper finds drag-coefficient behavior consistent with a roll-off at higher wind speeds rather than indefinite monotonic increase.
- The reported roll-off is qualitatively consistent with laboratory studies, modeling studies, and drag estimates inferred from log-profile methods.
- The authors also emphasize that both the magnitude of the roll-off and the wind speed at which it begins remain uncertain in this dataset.

# Limitations

- The direct measurements are limited to two storms and to winds below the major-hurricane regime.
- The analysis constrains momentum exchange only; enthalpy and moisture exchange require the companion papers.
- The paper explicitly notes uncertainty in both the threshold wind speed and the amount of drag-coefficient roll-off.
