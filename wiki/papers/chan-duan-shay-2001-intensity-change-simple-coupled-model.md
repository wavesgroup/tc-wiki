# Research Question

Chan et al. (2001) investigate whether a computationally simple coupled ocean-atmosphere framework can capture observed first-order intensity impacts from storm-induced SST cooling.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Simplified coupled modeling experiments targeted at intensity tendency.
- Core mechanism: Ocean mixed-layer cooling feeds back on surface enthalpy flux and storm intensification rate.
- Comparative emphasis: Sensitivity of intensity evolution to translation speed and ocean thermal structure under a lightweight coupled configuration.

# Findings

- Chan et al. (2001) found that even simple coupling can reproduce a meaningful intensity-damping effect from cold wakes.
- The study reinforced that storms over shallow warm layers or with slower forward speed are more susceptible to self-induced cooling penalties.
- Inference for this wiki: the paper supports the portability of cold-wake feedback concepts from high-complexity systems to reduced-order intensity models.
