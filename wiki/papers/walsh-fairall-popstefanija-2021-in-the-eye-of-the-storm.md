# Citation

Walsh, E. J., C. W. Fairall, and I. PopStefanija, 2021: *In the eye of the storm*. *Journal of Physical Oceanography*, 51, 1835-1842. DOI: [10.1175/JPO-D-20-0219.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/JPO-D-20-0219.1).

Accessed text sources: Open-access PDF via NOAA Repository and DOI bibliographic metadata.

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Walsh et al. (2021) evaluate whether NOAA's airborne Wide Swath Radar Altimeter (WSRA) can provide operationally useful, high-density directional wave-spectrum mapping across hurricane inner-core and near-core regions.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Instrumentation-focused observational paper with hurricane case demonstration.
- Platform: NOAA WP-3D aircraft carrying WSRA (16-GHz digital beamforming radar altimeter).
- Products: Significant wave height, directional wave spectra, primary/secondary wave-system direction and wavelength, rainfall rate, and sea-surface mean square slope.
- Demonstration case: Hurricane Lorenzo (29 September 2019), including 135 directional spectra in storm-relative coordinates.

# Findings

- The paper reports that WSRA can map dense, storm-scale wave-topography and directional-spectrum structure in near real time for hurricane operations.
- In the Lorenzo case, the largest waves were observed in the right-front quadrant, illustrating pronounced azimuthal asymmetry in wave conditions.
- Authors position WSRA as the operational successor to earlier airborne systems and explicitly connect it to the observational chain from [Wright et al. (2001)](wright-et-al-2001-hurricane-directional-wave-spectrum-open-ocean.md) and [Walsh et al. (2002)](walsh-et-al-2002-hurricane-directional-wave-spectrum-landfall.md).

# Limitations

- The paper is primarily an instrument-and-demonstration study, not a broad multi-storm climatology of hurricane wave spectra.
- Example diagnostics are case-based and sensitive to storm structure, weakening/strengthening phase, and flight-track geometry.
- Momentum/enthalpy flux implications are inferred rather than closed directly, so coupled exchange constraints still require dedicated flux studies.
