# Citation

Judt, F., and S. S. Chen, 2010: *Convectively generated potential vorticity in rainbands and formation of the secondary eyewall in Hurricane Rita of 2005*. *Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences*, 67(11), 3581-3599. DOI: [10.1175/2010JAS3471.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/2010JAS3471.1).

Accessed text source: [NSF NCAR/UCAR publication record + abstract](https://impacts.ucar.edu/en/publications/convectively-generated-potential-vorticity-in-rainbands-and-forma/).

Peer-review status: Peer-reviewed primary research article.

# Research Question

Judt and Chen (2010) ask how a secondary eyewall formed in Hurricane Rita (2005), whether convectively generated rainband potential vorticity (PV) was central to that process, and what role vortex Rossby waves (VRWs) played compared with a contrasting Hurricane Katrina (2005) case that retained a single eyewall.

# Data And Methods

- Study type: Comparative real-storm process study.
- Storms: Hurricanes Rita and Katrina (2005) during the Hurricane Rainband and Intensity Change Experiment (RAINEX) period.
- Observations: RAINEX aircraft measurements of the rainbands and inner core.
- Model setup: Triply nested high-resolution full-physics model, with 1.67-km grid spacing in the innermost domain, initialized from operational real-time forecasts.
- Diagnostics: Wind, vorticity, PV, and VRW activity in the inner-core and rainband regions.

# Findings

- Rita exhibited stronger PV generation and accumulation in the rainband region than Katrina, producing a secondary PV/vorticity maximum that later became the secondary eyewall.
- A pronounced moat developed between Rita's primary eyewall and outer convection ring, while Katrina maintained outward-propagating VRWs from the inner core toward the rainband region without forming a comparable secondary eyewall during the analyzed period.
- Judt and Chen (2010) argue that VRWs were not the initiating mechanism for Rita's secondary eyewall formation because the moat region had a near-zero PV gradient that did not support radial VRW propagation.
- The paper instead identifies large accumulation of convectively generated rainband PV as the key factor in the initial secondary-eyewall formation process for Rita.
- Inference for this wiki: secondary-eyewall formation should not be framed only as an inner-core wave problem; outer-core convective organization, PV aggregation, and moat structure can be first-order controls.

# Limitations

- This is a two-storm comparative study, so the generality of the diagnosed pathway remains uncertain.
- The analysis depends on a specific high-resolution forecast configuration and the fidelity of real-time initialized simulations.
- The accessible source here is publication-record abstract constrained; full-text ingestion would improve chronology, diagnostics, and separation between observed versus modeled evidence.
- The paper targets initial secondary-eyewall formation, not a full climatology of eyewall replacement cycles.
